Short history of money

Below is an attempt to reconstruct the flow of history of money. Why, and how, money were invented and evolved with time. Events are aligned in chronological order.

1. Tribal society

  • barter - exchange with no profit
  • tribes have small communication radius, no distant travel
  • no money, no wars, no literacy

2. Invention of a profitable trade: an unknown genius

  • trade with profit: the biggest invention in history
  • still barter, but TRADER exchanges with profit
  • trader travels, exchanges goods between distant tribes
  • indigenous tribals are easy to manipulate
  • no idea about the nominal cost of goods from distant places
  • profits are enourmous
  • formation of "trade houses" which proliferate fast
  • accumulation of knowledge and know-how across all trade routes

3. World expansion. Money. Capitalism.

  • trade with profit: the biggest invention in history
  • still barter, but TRADER exchanges with profit
  • trader travels, exchanges goods between distant tribes
  • aborigines have total trust, profits are enourmous
  • forming of "trade houses" which proliferate fast
  • beginning of literacy and maths, for accounting
  • Invention of a religion for traders (judaism)
  • Invention of a religion for aborigines (islam)
  • trader minority can use intellect to organise labor
  • true capitalism. hired workforce, e.g. Ghana - South America

4. Weapons. Gangs. States. Feudalism then slavery

  • traders start to use gold as money. gold mines around the world
  • aborigines are given shiny glass, not gold
  • establishment of the supreme court in Medina, for traders only
  • traders: interested in trade route secrecy and safety
  • establishment of bandits who rob traders and put them under control
  • thousands of short-lived ancient "states": gangs!
  • "state" impose taxes and territorial control
  • "states" against traders. too oppressive "states" make traders leave
  • Most anceint mines in Yemen. Elimination of Yemen woods to desert. Weapons
  • Weapons: make it possible for gangs to control majority by force, not by capitalism
  • the world divides on a ruling class (chosen) and the rest

  • Moshe (Moses): a judge of a supreme court of Medina
  • Idea: let the trader's court rules be for all, not just traders
  • Idea: whatever your religion is, your actions are judged by court, same for all
