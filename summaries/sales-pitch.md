# Sales Pitch by April Dunford

## Core idea
A sales pitch is not about presenting your product.  
It’s about guiding the buyer to make a confident decision.

## The problem with most pitches
Typical pitch:
- starts with product or demo
- dumps features
- reacts to questions

Why it fails:
- buyers don’t understand the problem space
- they compare you incorrectly
- default decision becomes “do nothing”

## Key insight
The best pitches reframe how the customer thinks about the problem, so your solution becomes the obvious choice.

## The structure of a great pitch

### 1. Setup (educate the buyer)
You control how the buyer evaluates options.

- Frame the problem
- Explain alternatives (including “do nothing”)
- Introduce a key insight (what others get wrong)
- Define the ideal solution

Goal: build a mental model in the buyer’s head

### 2. Follow-through (present your solution)
- Introduce your product
- Highlight differentiated value
- Provide proof (customers, data)
- Handle objections
- Drive next step

Goal: show you are the best choice in the model you created

## Differentiated value (critical concept)
Not:
- “We have feature X”

But:
- “We solve this problem in a way alternatives cannot”

You must answer:
- Why choose you vs competitors
- Why not build internally
- Why not do nothing

## Common mistakes
- Demo too early
- Feature-first narrative
- No clear alternatives
- No clear “why now”
- Ignoring “do nothing”

## Example story

### Bad pitch
“We’re a real-time analytics platform using streaming pipelines…”

Customer reaction:
- “Sounds complex”
- “Do I even need this?”

### Good pitch (reframed)
“There are three ways to handle real-time data:
1. Batch processing - simple but slow
2. Custom pipelines - flexible but complex
3. Managed streaming - fast but expensive

Most teams underestimate how hard custom pipelines become.

The ideal solution gives real-time insights without infrastructure overhead.

That’s exactly what we do.”

Result:
- buyer understands the space
- sees tradeoffs
- sees why you win

## “Do nothing” is your biggest competitor
Customers often choose:
- delay
- stick with current workflow

Your pitch must answer:
- why change?
- why now?

## Practical checklist
A strong pitch answers:

1. What problem space are we in?
2. What are the alternatives?
3. What’s the key insight?
4. What does a perfect solution look like?
5. How is your product different?
6. Why should I believe you?
7. Why act now?

## TL;DR
- Don’t pitch your product
- Teach the customer how to evaluate solutions
- Control the comparison
- Then show why you win
